05 Methodology

Matching Algorithm & Decision Pipeline

Configurable weighted scoring with hard filters, confidence factor, and three-outcome classification.

Score Formula (Default Weights)
Overall Score = 0.50 × Skill Score
                + 0.30 × Experience Score
                + 0.20 × Education Score
                × Confidence Factor

Skill Score = 0.70 × (matched_req / total_req)
               + 0.30 × (matched_pref / total_pref)

Exp. Score = min(1.0, candidate_yrs / required_yrs)
Edu. Score = 1.0 match | 0.5 any degree | 0.0 none
Hard Filters (auto-Reject)
  • Minimum experience not met
  • Mandatory skills threshold not reached
  • Overqualification guard (> 3 years excess)

Why F1-Score as Primary Metric?
Precision = TP / (TP + FP)
Recall = TP / (TP + FN)
F1-Score = 2 × P × R / (P + R)

F1 balances both extraction completeness
(recall) and accuracy (precision) in a
single interpretable number [0–1].
Ground Truth
9 hand-annotated resumes spanning: experienced engineers, freshers, career changers, overqualified PhDs, employment gaps, DevOps specialists, Unicode names, and review-borderline candidates.

09 Drawbacks & Limitations

Known Limitations

Honest assessment of current system boundaries and areas requiring further research.

Keyword-Based Only

Rule-based matching misses semantic synonyms (e.g., "CI/CD" vs. "continuous integration"). No BERT/embedding similarity yet.

English Only

System is limited to English-language resumes and job descriptions. Multi-language NER would require additional models.

Limited Evaluation Scale

10 annotated pairs is meaningful but insufficient for publication-grade statistical significance. Target: 50+ across domains.

Certification F1 = 0.75

Certification extraction regex only catches common patterns. Niche certifications with non-standard formats are missed.

SMTP Firewall Issues

Corporate networks block ports 587/465. Requires Gmail App Password or mobile hotspot workaround.

Batch Size Cap (10)

Processing is sequential within a Streamlit session. Enterprise-grade scaling would require async workers or a job queue.
